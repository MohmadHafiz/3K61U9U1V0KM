Why & how are we doing this?

The Problems We Are Addressing

According to a report by United Nation on 2005, In developing countries, 80% to 90% of persons with disabilities of working age are unemployed, whereas in industrialized countries the figure is between 50% and 70%. More over in Asia Pacific, there are 370 million persons with disabilities, 238 million of them of working age. Their unemployment rate is usually double that of the general population and often as high as 80% or more.

The Solutions

Business Education
Provide comprehensive business education that combines hard skills and soft skills training to empower individuals with disabilities to succeed in entrepreneurship.
Market Access
Facilitate market access for members and alumni by providing a dedicated marketplace where they can advertise and sell their products and services to a wider audience.
Networking and Partnerships
Connect members with professionals, experts, investors, and potential partners to foster collaborations, mentorship opportunities, and access to funding.
Virtual Expo
Offer a virtual expo platform where members can showcase their products and services in an immersive and engaging manner, allowing potential customers to experience their offerings virtually.
Online Training and Workshops
Provide online training courses, workshops, and live support sessions to enhance members' business skills, knowledge, and capabilities.
Mentorship and Support
Offer mentorship programs and a supportive community where members can receive guidance, advice, and support throughout their entrepreneurial journey.
Sustainable and Inclusive Approach
Promote sustainability and reduce inequality by creating equal opportunities for individuals with disabilities to start and grow their businesses, fostering an inclusive and diverse entrepreneurial ecosystem.
